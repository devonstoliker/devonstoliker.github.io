PsiConnect Study

  • Status: Complete, with multiple outputs under review.
  • Australia’s first psychedelic imaging trial, investigating interactions between psilocybin, brain connectivity, context and behaviour in healthy adults.
  • World’s largest fMRI dataset collected under psychedelics to date by sample size (N = 65).
  • Extensive baseline imaging and imaging under the effects of 19 mg psilocybin using both MRI and EEG, with before and longitudinal after behavioural measures.
  • Large supportive interdisciplinary team including Leonardo Novelli, Moein Khajehnejad, and Adeel Razi.
  • This research reveals a subject-level signature of the transformative psychedelic state and advanced how we understand context-dependent reorganisation of brain networks during the psychedelic state.

  • Data descriptor: PsiConnect: A Multimodal Neuroimaging Dataset of Context-Dependent Brain and Behaviour Dynamics under Psilocybin

Keio University Psilocybin Trial

Lead investigators: Professor Hiroyuki Uchida and Keisuke Kusudo

  • Japan’s first serotonergic psychedelic clinical trial, evaluating safety and efficacy of psilocybin in treatment-resistant depression, with low (10 mg) and high (25 mg) doses across multiple sessions.
  • Advisory role on trial design, emphasising mindset and contextual factors. Curated therapeutic music playlists tailored to each dosing session.
  • Status: Implementation phase.
