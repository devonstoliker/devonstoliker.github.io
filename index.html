About

I'm a neuroscientist and researcher working at the intersection of perception, psychedelic science, and artificial intelligence. I use multimodal neuroimaging, machine learning, and computational modelling to study states of consciousness and brain network dynamics.

Research

My work focuses on combining predictive processing, dynamic causal modelling, and data-driven methods (e.g. CEBRA, embeddings) to understand how psychedelics modulate sensory, self-related, and affective brain circuits. I lead the PsiConnect study, the largest neuroimaging dataset collected under psilocybin to date.
