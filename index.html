About Me

I’m a neuroscientist exploring how psychedelics alter brain connectivity and conscious experience. My work bridges computational modelling, machine learning, and multimodal neuroimaging (fMRI, EEG) to understand the neural dynamics underlying selfhood, perception, and therapeutic change.

I lead the PsiConnect study, the largest fMRI dataset collected under psilocybin to date. This research has advanced how we understand context-dependent reorganisation of brain networks during altered states. I also collaborate with international partners to develop predictive models of individual responses to psychedelics.

My current focus includes applying dimensionality reduction, CEBRA-based embeddings, and effective connectivity modelling to study large-scale brain dynamics in relation to subjective experience.
